# Copy Check

Use for copywriting, social posts, website text, landing page sections, activity announcements, product blurbs, and public-facing text.

## Inspect For

- audience: who needs to understand or act
- promise: what is offered, learned, received, or changed
- clarity: concrete nouns, direct verbs, no vague claims
- structure: headline, context, value, details, CTA
- trust: dates, price, location, eligibility, host, proof, limitations
- tone: matches audience, channel, brand, and urgency
- risk: overclaim, ambiguity, missing condition, unclear next step

## Common Risks

- headline is clever but not informative
- CTA does not say what happens next
- important details appear too late
- too many abstract adjectives
- target audience is unclear
- event or offer details are missing
- claim sounds stronger than evidence

## Output Notes

Compare the current copy with a clearer version. Keep rewrites compact unless the user asks for a full rewrite. Flag factual gaps instead of inventing facts.
