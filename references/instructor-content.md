# Instructor Content

Use for instructor, guest, speaker, course teacher, partner, collaborator, or organization introductions.

## Inspect For

- identity: correct name, title, organization, role
- relevance: why this person matters for this course, event, or collaboration
- credibility: experience, achievements, representative work, proof points
- audience fit: why participants should care
- scope: what they will teach, share, host, judge, or contribute
- tone: respectful, specific, not inflated
- permissions: photos, titles, credentials, and claims are safe to use

## Common Risks

- intro is a generic bio and does not connect to the event
- title or organization is outdated
- achievements are too broad or unverifiable
- too many credentials, not enough relevance
- missing role in the activity
- tone feels exaggerated

Use the comparison table to turn generic bio lines into audience-relevant introduction lines. If facts are missing, mark them as items to confirm instead of filling them in.
