# Daily Life

Use for everyday pre-action checks: leaving home, bedtime, errands, doctor visits, documents, travel, returning home, or daily wrap-up.

## Inspect For

- must-have items: phone, wallet, keys, ID, cards, tickets, medication, charger
- timing risks: appointment time, commute time, alarm, closing hours, deadline
- location risks: address, floor, counter, meeting point, link, route
- document risks: ID, health card, copies, photos, signatures, payment, next step
- home risks: door, windows, appliances, gas, lights, pets, trash, food
- recovery risks: items that are hard to fix once the user leaves

## Common Check Types

- leaving home: phone, wallet/card/cash, keys, umbrella/coat, transit card, door/home safety
- bedtime: alarm, charging, tomorrow's first item, must-bring objects, doors/windows
- errands/documents: ID, required documents, copies/photos, appointment, payment, next step
- doctor visit: health card, appointment, question list, medication list, payment, follow-up
- travel: ID/passport, tickets/booking, medication, charger, money, home safety
- returning home: keys/wallet back in place, charging, receipts/documents, tomorrow prep

## Output Notes

Keep the final checklist to 5 to 9 items. Put the hardest-to-recover items first. For official documents or medical logistics, remind the user to verify official requirements without giving professional advice.
