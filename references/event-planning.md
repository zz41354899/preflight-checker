# Event Planning

Use for courses, activities, workshops, programs, proposals, rundowns, planning documents, and participant-facing event information.

## Inspect For

- purpose: what the event is for and what success means
- audience: who should attend and what they need beforehand
- offer: topic, outcome, format, level, deliverable
- logistics: date, time, timezone, location, link, capacity, price, registration
- roles: host, facilitator, assistant, sponsor, organizer, contact
- flow: agenda, transitions, break time, Q&A, setup, teardown
- materials: slides, handouts, tools, equipment, access
- risks: missing approval, unclear refund/cancel rule, accessibility, contingency

## Common Risks

- audience and level are unclear
- registration CTA or deadline is missing
- schedule has no setup or buffer time
- host or facilitator role is vague
- location/link is absent or inconsistent
- no owner for materials, reminders, or follow-up
- promise is bigger than the format can support

## Output Notes

Prioritize missing logistics and unclear participant expectations. For public events, do not invent dates, prices, organizers, sponsors, or policies.
